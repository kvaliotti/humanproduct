# Unique Attribute Categories

When isolating unique attributes (Step 5), systematically explore each category. Most teams only think of product features and miss 4-5 other categories.

## 1. Technical Features

What the product can DO that alternatives cannot.

**Probe questions:**
- What specific capabilities exist that no alternative offers?
- What integrations are unique?
- What algorithms, models, or technical approaches are proprietary?
- What performance benchmarks do you hit that alternatives don't?

**Examples:**
- "Patented fuzzy logic query algorithm"
- "Native integration with QuickBooks, Xero, and FreshBooks"
- "Sub-100ms response time on datasets over 100M rows"
- "Real-time collaboration with conflict resolution"

## 2. Delivery Model

HOW the product is delivered and consumed.

**Probe questions:**
- SaaS vs. on-premise vs. hybrid?
- Self-serve vs. managed vs. white-glove?
- Mobile-first vs. desktop vs. both?
- API-first vs. UI-first?
- Implementation time compared to alternatives?

**Examples:**
- "Fully self-serve — live in under 5 minutes, no implementation needed"
- "On-premise deployment for air-gapped environments"
- "Mobile-native — works offline, syncs when connected"

## 3. Business Model

How the product is PRICED and SOLD.

**Probe questions:**
- Free tier or freemium?
- Usage-based vs. seat-based vs. flat rate?
- No long-term contract?
- Different payment terms?
- Bundled with services?

**Examples:**
- "Free for up to 5 users — no credit card required"
- "Pay-per-use, no minimum commitment"
- "Includes 10 hours of implementation consulting"

## 4. Expertise & Experience

What the TEAM knows that alternatives' teams don't.

**Probe questions:**
- Does the team have unique industry experience?
- Former practitioners in the customer's domain?
- Published research or thought leadership?
- Certifications or credentials?
- Track record with specific customer types?

**Examples:**
- "Founded by 3 former investment bankers who lived this problem"
- "Team includes 2 published researchers in NLP"
- "15 years of compliance experience in healthcare"

## 5. Partnerships & Ecosystem

Who you're CONNECTED to that alternatives aren't.

**Probe questions:**
- Exclusive technology partnerships?
- Channel or reseller relationships?
- Data partnerships?
- Certification or endorsement from key players?

**Examples:**
- "Exclusive data partnership with [industry association]"
- "Only product certified by [regulatory body]"
- "Pre-integrated into [major platform] marketplace"

## 6. Team Composition

The unique COMBINATION of skills on the team.

**Probe questions:**
- What disciplines are represented?
- What's the unusual combination?
- What's the depth in each area?

**Examples:**
- "Team combines ML engineers + licensed psychologists — no competitor has both"
- "Former operators from 3 of the top 5 companies in this space"

## Consideration vs. Retention Attributes

| Type | When It Matters | Focus For Positioning? |
|------|----------------|----------------------|
| Consideration | Purchase decision | ✅ YES — this is what positioning is about |
| Retention | Renewal decision | ❌ NO — save for customer success, not positioning |

**Consideration examples:** Core functionality, unique capabilities, price, implementation speed
**Retention examples:** Customer support quality, account management, ease of doing business

Customers who don't see enough value to BUY will never experience your retention attributes.
