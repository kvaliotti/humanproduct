# Competitive Alternatives Elicitation Guide

## Why "Alternatives" Not "Competitors"

Research on two approaches with customers:
1. Asking about problems → descriptions were too narrow and technical
2. Asking about alternatives → revealed how customers actually categorize and evaluate

**Example:** Database users said their problem was "run a fast query." But when asked what they'd use instead, none named another database — they named BI tools and data warehouses. The competitive alternative revealed the true market frame.

## The Full Elicitation Sequence

### Round 1: Open-Ended
"If our product didn't exist tomorrow, what would your best customers do instead?"

Let the user brainstorm freely. Don't filter yet.

### Round 2: Category Prompts
For each category they might have missed:

**General-purpose tools:**
"Would any of your customers solve this with Excel, Google Sheets, Notion, Airtable, or another general tool?"

**Manual/human processes:**
"Could they hire someone to do this manually? An intern? A contractor? A consultant?"

**Status quo / do nothing:**
"What happens if they just don't solve this problem? Is the pain bearable?"

**Cobbled combinations:**
"Would they duct-tape together 2-3 tools? What combination?"

**Internal builds:**
"Would they build something in-house? Has anyone tried?"

### Round 3: Validation
For each alternative:
"Have your actual customers told you this? Or is this your inference?"

Best: "Multiple customers have said they'd use X."
Acceptable: "Based on what we see in sales conversations."
Weak: "I think they would." → Flag as assumption.

### Round 4: Ranking
"Which of these is the MOST common? What would the majority of your best-fit customers default to?"

Rank from most to least common. Group into natural clusters.

## Alternative Types with Examples

| Type | B2B Examples | Consumer Examples |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| Direct competitor | Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive | Spotify, Apple Music |
| Adjacent tool | Excel + Zapier, Google Sheets + manual | Paper notebook, phone notes app |
| Manual process | Hire analyst, consultant, intern | Do it yourself, ask a friend |
| Do nothing | Live with the pain, deprioritize | Go without, find workaround |
| Combination | Email + spreadsheet + weekly meeting | Reddit + YouTube + trial and error |
| Internal build | Custom script, internal tool | N/A |

## Common Failure Modes

### "We don't have competitors"
Response: "Everyone has alternatives. Your customers are doing SOMETHING today without your product. What is it?"

### Only listing funded startups
Response: "Those are companies in your space. But have your customers heard of them? What are your customers CURRENTLY using?"

### Listing aspirational competitors
Response: "You'd like to compete with Salesforce. But do your actual best-fit customers consider Salesforce? Or is their real alternative something smaller?"

### Forgetting "do nothing"
Response: "For many products, the #1 competitor is inertia. What percentage of your pipeline is lost to 'do nothing' or 'not now'? If significant, that's a competitive alternative."
