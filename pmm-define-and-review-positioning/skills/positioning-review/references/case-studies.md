# Positioning Case Studies

Cite these when diagnosing positioning problems that parallel a known case.

## Database → Data Warehouse
**Problem diagnosed:** Positioned in wrong category (database vs. data warehouse). Couldn't compete with Oracle.
**Lesson:** If your category pits you against a giant, an adjacent category with no leader may be the right frame.

## Janna Systems: CRM → CRM for Investment Banks
**Problem diagnosed:** Generic CRM positioning forced feature-by-feature comparison with Siebel ($2B). Always lost.
**Lesson:** Big Fish, Small Pond. Same product, narrower frame = $2M to $70M in 18 months. Acquired for $1.7B.

## Arm & Hammer: Baking Soda → Deodorizer
**Problem diagnosed:** Clung to original identity ("baking product") even as market shifted away from home baking.
**Lesson:** Letting go of positioning baggage unlocked $16M → $318M growth.

## Clearpath Robotics → OTTO Motors
**Problem diagnosed:** "Robot" triggered wrong assumptions (stationary, repetitive-task machines). Product was mobile, autonomous.
**Lesson:** Category name triggers feature expectations. If the name misleads, the category is wrong.

## Eloqua: Created "Marketing Automation"
**Problem diagnosed:** No existing category made their strengths obvious.
**Lesson:** Category creation works when product is genuinely new — but it's the most expensive and risky path. $12M → $96M → $870M acquisition.

## Wave Accounting → Wave Financial Services
**Problem diagnosed:** "Accounting" in the name narrowed perception. Customers wanted invoicing, payments, payroll.
**Lesson:** When product scope outgrows the category, the category must expand too.

## Redgate Software + DevOps
**Problem diagnosed:** Strong category position but not seen as strategic/urgent.
**Lesson:** Trend layering (not trend replacing) adds urgency. "Database DevOps" = 100% increase in inbound leads.
