# Positioning Anti-Patterns

Use this as a real-time checker during the workshop. When you detect an anti-pattern, call it out immediately with the specific fix.

## The 9 Common Mistakes

| # | Mistake | How to Detect | Fix |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Starting with features instead of competitive alternatives | User leads with "our product does X, Y, Z" without mentioning what customers use today | Redirect: "Let's pause features. What would your best customers use if you didn't exist?" |
| 2 | Positioning for investors, not customers | Language like "disruptive", "paradigm shift", "10x improvement"; features described in vision terms, not immediate-value terms | Challenge: "Investors buy future vision. Customers buy immediate value for an immediate pain. What's the immediate pain?" |
| 3 | Using a positioning statement template | User tries to fill in "FOR [target], our [product] IS A [category]..." | Explain: templates assume you know the answers. The 10-step process helps you discover them. |
| 4 | Surveying all customers instead of only the best | User cites NPS surveys, average satisfaction, or "most customers say..." | Redirect: "Filter to your top 10% — the ones who bought fast, never discount, and refer. What do THEY say?" |
| 5 | Treating default positioning as the only option | "We're an X because that's what we built" / resistance to considering other frames | Challenge: "Every product can be positioned in multiple markets. The one you built for may not be the best one." |
| 6 | Trying to be everything to everyone | "Our target is any company with data" / resistance to narrowing | Push: "Narrow ≠ small. It eliminates prospects who were never going to buy. Can you hit near-term targets with just your best-fit segment?" |
| 7 | Layering trends without market context | "We're an AI-powered..." without first declaring what category | Redirect: "Market first, trend second. What are you WITHOUT the trend? Start there." |
| 8 | Never revisiting positioning | "We did positioning two years ago" as if it's permanent | Flag: "Markets shift. Check every 6 months or after major competitor/regulation/technology changes." |
| 9 | Making it a marketing-only exercise | "Marketing is handling the positioning" | Escalate: "Positioning is business strategy. The CEO/founder must lead. Cross-functional team must buy in, or it won't stick." |

## Step-Specific Anti-Patterns

### Step 4 (Competitive Alternatives)
- **Listing startup rivals customers have never heard of.** Customers' yardstick is what they use TODAY, not what VCs fund.
- **Asking "what problem are you solving?"** Too narrow. Ask "what would you use instead?" to reveal actual comparison set.
- **Projecting expert landscape knowledge onto customers.** Customers often have never purchased a solution like yours before.

### Step 5 (Unique Attributes)
- **Confusing attributes with value.** "Saves time" is value. "One-click report generation" is an attribute.
- **Listing unprovable claims.** "Best UX" is opinion. "Setup takes under 2 minutes with zero configuration" is fact.
- **Only listing product features.** Explore delivery model, business model, expertise, partnerships, team skills.

### Step 6 (Value Mapping)
- **Treating features and benefits as interchangeable.** "We have a patented algorithm" → customer says "So what?" Bridge to what it means for them.
- **Too many themes.** 1-4 value clusters max. More = unfocused positioning.

### Step 7 (Who Cares)
- **Weak segmentation.** "SMBs" or "enterprise" or "product teams" — not actionable. Push for characteristics that predict love-at-first-use.
- **Segment too broad to build a prospect list.** If you can't name 10 real accounts that fit, it's too broad.

### Step 8 (Market Frame)
- **Defaulting to Head to Head when Big Fish, Small Pond is better.** Most startups should NOT compete head-on with incumbents.
- **Creating a new category when an existing one works.** Category creation is the nuclear option — expensive, slow, risky.
- **Choosing a market that triggers wrong assumptions.** If prospects expect features you don't have, the category is wrong.

### Step 9 (Trends)
- **Trend without market.** "Sharing economy for pets" — nobody knows what you do.
- **Trend without product link.** Long Island Iced Tea → Long Blockchain. Stock crashed, delisted.
- **Trend-driven content without connection.** Blogging about AI without linking to your solution attracts readers, sells nothing.
