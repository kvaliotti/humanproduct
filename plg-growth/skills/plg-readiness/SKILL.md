# PLG Readiness Assessment

## Purpose

You conduct a comprehensive assessment of whether product-led growth is viable for a product, how mature the PLG foundations are, and what customers actually value (decision-driver research). You produce a PLG Readiness Scorecard with per-dimension scores, gap analysis, and prioritized recommendations.

This skill merges PLG suitability assessment with decision-driver research (originally plg-positioning). The insight: you cannot assess PLG fit without understanding what drives customer decisions, because PLG only works if the product can prove its top value drivers through the free/trial experience.

## When to Invoke

Trigger phrases:
- "is PLG right for my product"
- "PLG readiness" / "PLG fit assessment"
- "should we go product-led"
- "PLG maturity" / "evaluate PLG fit"
- "PLG positioning" / "what do customers value"
- "decision drivers" / "positioning research"
- "why do customers choose us"
- "data-informed positioning"

## Structured Problem-Solving Backbone

1. **Issue Trees (MECE)** — decompose readiness into 6 independent dimensions
2. **Hypothesis Trees** — hypothesize fit/gaps before gathering evidence
3. **Driver Disaggregation** — break "readiness" into measurable components
4. **80/20 Prioritization** — focus remediation on 2-3 highest-impact gaps
5. **So-What Synthesis (Minto Pyramid)** — lead with the verdict, then the evidence
6. **Hypothesis-Driven Work Plans** — every recommendation ties to a hypothesis about what will improve fit

## Step-by-Step Methodology

### Step 1: Evaluate Product Against 4 PLG Suitability Criteria

Load the suitability criteria:
> Read `references/suitability-criteria.md`

Score the product on each criterion (1-5 scale):

| Criterion | What to Assess |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Self-serve capability** | Can a user sign up, set up, and get value without human help? |
| **Low barrier to start** | Is entry free/cheap and fast? Or does it require procurement, implementation, data migration? |
| **Value demonstrable pre-purchase** | Can the user experience the core value before paying? How fast is aha moment? |
| **Natural expansion dynamics** | Does usage naturally spread? Viral, network effects, seat-based, usage-based expansion? |

**Scoring rubric:**
- 5 = Strong PLG fit on this dimension
- 4 = Good fit with minor friction
- 3 = Possible but requires significant investment
- 2 = Weak fit, major barriers
- 1 = Fundamentally incompatible

**Aggregate:** Average >= 4.0 = strong PLG candidate. 3.0-3.9 = viable with investment. < 3.0 = PLG as secondary motion only.

### Step 2: Assess the 3 Pillars of PLG

Load the pillar assessment guide:
> Read `references/three-pillars-assessment.md`

#### Pillar 1: Design for the End User
- **Easy to understand:** Can a new user grasp what the product does in <30 seconds?
- **Easy to use:** Can they complete the core workflow without training?
- **Easy to purchase:** Can they upgrade/pay without talking to sales?

#### Pillar 2: Deliver Value Before You Capture Value
- **Activation:** Is there a clear aha moment? What % of signups reach it?
- **Time to value (TTV):** How long from signup to first value? Minutes? Hours? Days?
- **Value-capture ratio:** How much value does the free experience deliver vs. what's locked behind paywall?

#### Pillar 3: Product with Go-To-Market Intent (TASE)
- **Traffic:** Does the product generate its own traffic? (SEO content, viral sharing, embeds, integrations)
- **Activation:** Is onboarding optimized as a growth lever, not just a feature?
- **Stickiness:** Does the product build habit and switching cost through use?
- **Expansion:** Does individual use naturally expand to team/org use?

Score each sub-dimension 1-5. Identify the weakest links.

### Step 3: Test PMF Signals

Assess product-market fit through 4 lenses:

#### 3a. Sean Ellis Test
- Ask: "If you surveyed users 'How would you feel if you could no longer use [product]?', what % say 'very disappointed'?"
- Threshold: 40%+ = strong PMF. 25-40% = emerging. <25% = pre-PMF.
- If they have not run this survey: recommend it as immediate action.

#### 3b. Retention Curve Shape
- Does the retention curve flatten? At what level? After how long?
- Flattening above 20% (B2B) or 10% (B2C) at D90+ = healthy.
- Still declining at D90 = retention problem before PLG investment.

#### 3c. Usage Frequency vs. Natural Frequency
- How often should the product be used based on the use case? (Daily tool? Weekly review? Monthly report?)
- Does actual usage match or exceed natural frequency?
- Gap between natural and actual = engagement problem.

#### 3d. Depth of Engagement
- Are users using multiple features or just one?
- Is usage deepening over time (more features, more data, more integrations)?
- Shallow, single-feature usage = risk of commoditization.

### Step 4: Compare Growth Model (SLG vs MLG vs PLG)

Load the comparison matrix:
> Read `references/growth-model-comparison.md`

Map the product's current reality against all three models across dimensions:
- Deal size and complexity
- Buyer persona and authority
- Sales cycle length
- CAC and payback period
- Expansion mechanism
- Market maturity

**Key question:** Is the product currently using the RIGHT growth model, or is there a mismatch? Many products are running SLG when PLG would be more efficient (or vice versa).

Produce a recommendation: Pure PLG, PLG-primary with sales assist, Hybrid (PLG + SLG), SLG-primary with PLG motion, or SLG-only.

### Step 5: Assess Competitive Moat Potential

Load the moat framework:
> Read `references/competitive-moats.md`

Evaluate three moat categories:

#### 5a. Network Effects
- **Direct:** Does product get better with more users on the same side? (Communication tools, social platforms)
- **Indirect:** Does it get better with more users on the other side? (Marketplaces, platforms)
- **Data:** Does it get better as more data flows through it? (Analytics, AI/ML products)

#### 5b. Switching Costs
- How much data/content/workflows would a user lose by switching?
- How many integrations connect through the product?
- How much team training/habit would need to be rebuilt?

#### 5c. Data Moat / Ecosystem Lock-in
- Does the product accumulate proprietary data that increases value?
- Is it becoming a system of record?
- Does it sit at a workflow hub connecting multiple tools?

**PLG relevance:** Strong moats make PLG more defensible. Weak moats mean competitors can replicate your free experience — you need to build moats deliberately.

### Step 6: Decision-Driver Research

This is the merged positioning research methodology. Load the full guide:
> Read `references/decision-driver-research.md`

#### 6a. Define the Decision
- Who is the ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)?
- What decision are they making? (Adopt a new tool? Switch from competitor? Expand usage?)
- Who is involved in the decision? (End user? Manager? Procurement?)

#### 6b. Discover Drivers (Interview-Based)
If the user has access to customers/prospects, guide them through wide interviews:
- "What was your first research action when you realized you needed [category]?"
- "Who were the stakeholders in the decision?"
- "Why did you ultimately select [product/competitor]?"
- "How did you compare options?"
- "What was the second-best option and why didn't you choose it?"

Extract the raw list of decision drivers from interview patterns.

#### 6c. Rank Drivers (Survey-Based)
For each identified driver:
- Rate importance on Likert scale 1-7
- For top drivers, assess 5 dimensions:
  - **Meaning:** What exactly does this driver mean to the buyer?
  - **Indicators:** What evidence/proof do they look for?
  - **Measurement:** How do they evaluate whether a product delivers on this?
  - **Comparison:** How do they compare products on this dimension?
  - **Credibility:** What makes a claim about this driver believable?

#### 6d. Statistical Synthesis
- Calculate Average, Median, and Standard Deviation for each driver
- High average + low StdDev = universally important driver (table stakes)
- High average + high StdDev = important but segment-dependent (differentiation opportunity)
- Mid-range average + high StdDev = segmentation signal (different ICPs value it differently)

#### 6e. Map Drivers to PLG Mechanics
Critical synthesis step:
- **Do the top 3 decision drivers align with what the free/trial experience demonstrates?**
- If #1 driver is "reliability over time" but trial is 7 days — misalignment.
- If #1 driver is "ease of use" and free tier shows this instantly — strong PLG fit.
- **Can activation prove the #1 driver?** If yes, PLG has natural conversion power. If not, you need to redesign activation or adjust model.

## Inputs Required from User

- Product description and target user
- Current growth model and metrics (if available)
- Customer interview data or survey results (for decision-driver research; can be gathered as action item)
- Competitive landscape overview
- Team and resource constraints

## Anti-Patterns

- **Skipping PMF assessment:** Do not recommend PLG investment for pre-PMF products. Help them find PMF first.
- **Ignoring decision drivers:** Suitability criteria alone are insufficient. A product can score well on suitability but fail at PLG because the free experience does not prove what customers actually care about.
- **Binary thinking:** PLG readiness is not yes/no. It is a spectrum with specific gaps to close. Always identify the PATH to readiness, not just the current state.
- **Ignoring hybrid models:** Most successful companies blend PLG with other motions. Pure PLG is rare at scale.
