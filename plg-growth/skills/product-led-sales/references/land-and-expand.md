# Land-and-Expand Playbook

The PLS expansion journey from individual adoption to enterprise contract. Each stage has distinct triggers, sales actions, and success metrics.

---

## Stage 1: LAND — Individual / Team Adoption

### What Happens
A single user or small team adopts the product through self-serve. This is the "land" — the initial foothold inside the organization.

### Sub-Stages

**Individual Adoption:**
- Single user signs up (often without company approval)
- Uses product for personal workflow
- Gets value from individual use case
- This is the seed of all future expansion

**First Team Adoption:**
- Individual invites 2-5 teammates
- Team uses product for a shared workflow
- Team establishes regular usage patterns
- Product becomes embedded in team processes

### Triggers That Indicate Successful Landing
- User active for 7+ consecutive days
- First colleague invited
- First shared project/workspace created
- Core use case established (regular usage pattern detected)
- Positive engagement signals (feature depth, session length)

### Sales Action at This Stage
**None.** Do not contact users during the Land stage. Let self-serve work. Product and onboarding should handle this entirely.

Exception: If the user signed up from an account that is already a PQA (known enterprise target), notify the account team for awareness only — no outreach.

### Success Metrics
- Signup → Activation rate
- Activation → Team invite rate
- Time from signup to first team use
- Team adoption rate (% of invites that become active)

## Stage 2: EXPAND HORIZONTALLY — Cross-Department Spread

### What Happens
The product spreads from the initial team to other teams and departments within the organization. This is organic expansion — driven by internal word-of-mouth, shared projects, or visible success.

### How Horizontal Expansion Occurs

**Internal word-of-mouth:**
- Team A uses the product successfully
- Team A member mentions it to Team B colleague
- Team B tries it for their own use case

**Cross-team collaboration:**
- Team A shares a project or document with Team B
- Team B needs an account to participate
- Team B sees the value and adopts for their own work

**Visible success:**
- Team A's output (reports, dashboards, designs) is visible to other teams
- Other teams ask "what tool is that?"
- They sign up to replicate the success

**Champion advocacy:**
- Power user actively evangelizes the product internally
- Runs training sessions or demos for other teams
- Creates templates or processes others can follow

### Triggers That Indicate Horizontal Expansion
- New users signing up from the same company domain but different departments
- Shared projects or resources across teams
- New use cases detected (different from the original team's use case)
- Organic referrals from existing users to new departments
- Growth in active users without marketing touch

### Sales Actions at This Stage

**Monitor and prepare (do not over-engage):**
- Track multi-department spread in CRM
- Identify the champion(s) driving expansion
- Note the use cases per department

**Gentle engagement:**
- Offer the champion resources to help internal rollout (templates, best practices, internal pitch deck)
- "I noticed [Product] has spread to your design and marketing teams. Would it be helpful if we put together a quick best-practices guide for cross-team use?"
- Host a user community call or webinar for the company's users

**Do NOT:**
- Contact every new department directly (respect the organic spread)
- Push enterprise upgrade prematurely (let adoption deepen first)
- Bypass the champion to reach their leadership

### Success Metrics
- Number of departments using the product per account
- Cross-team collaboration frequency
- Time from first department to second department adoption
- Champion-assisted vs organic spread ratio

## Stage 3: EXPAND VERTICALLY — Tier Upgrade

### What Happens
The account outgrows the current plan and needs higher-tier features, higher limits, or organizational capabilities. This is the primary revenue expansion event.

### Vertical Expansion Triggers

**Feature Limits Hit:**
- Seat limit reached (team outgrew the plan)
- Storage/usage limit reached
- Project/workspace limit reached
- API rate limit exceeded
- These are the strongest upgrade signals

**Admin/Governance Features Needed:**
- Admin requests user management capabilities
- Team needs role-based access control
- Compliance requires audit logging
- Organization needs single sign-on (SSO/SAML)
- Data residency or security requirements

**Advanced Feature Discovery:**
- Users exploring advanced reporting/analytics
- Automation workflows hitting free tier limits
- Custom integrations requiring API access
- Priority support needed for critical workflows

**Volume Pricing Requested:**
- Account has many users and wants volume discount
- Multiple teams want to consolidate billing
- Finance team requests invoice/PO billing

### Sales Actions at This Stage

**Proactive outreach triggered by signals:**
- Limit-hit signal → SDR outreach within 24 hours
- Admin feature exploration → SDR outreach within 48 hours
- SSO/security request → AE + SE engagement immediately

**Upgrade conversations:**
1. Acknowledge current usage and success
2. Reference the specific trigger (limit, feature need)
3. Present the upgrade path clearly
4. Provide ROI justification using their actual data
5. Offer a trial of the higher tier (if applicable)

**Proposal approach:**
- Based on actual team size (not estimated)
- Include usage data as value evidence
- Compare current plan limitations vs proposed plan capabilities
- Include implementation support for enterprise features

### Success Metrics
- Time from limit hit to upgrade
- Upgrade conversion rate by trigger type
- Average revenue increase per upgrade
- Voluntary vs sales-assisted upgrade ratio

## Stage 4: ENTERPRISE CONVERSION — Bottom-Up to Top-Down

### What Happens
The organic, bottom-up adoption has created enough critical mass that the organization formalizes the relationship through an enterprise agreement. This is the largest revenue event and the culmination of the land-and-expand journey.

### Enterprise Conversion Triggers

**Scale triggers:**
- X+ active users across Y+ departments
- Product is embedded in critical workflows
- Multiple teams have independently adopted
- Total spend across individual plans is significant

**Procurement triggers:**
- Security review requested (SOC2, penetration test)
- SSO/SAML requirement (IT policy)
- Data processing agreement requested (legal/compliance)
- Volume pricing or enterprise discount requested (finance)
- Purchase order or invoice billing required (procurement)
- Vendor consolidation initiative

**Champion escalation triggers:**
- Champion requests help pitching internally
- Champion asks for executive presentation materials
- Champion needs ROI data for business case
- Champion's manager or VP reaches out

### Sales Actions at This Stage

**Build the business case with the champion:**
1. Compile usage data across all teams
2. Calculate total current spend (fragmented individual plans)
3. Show consolidated enterprise pricing (often lower per-user)
4. Quantify value delivered (projects completed, time saved, etc.)
5. Provide security/compliance documentation proactively

**Engage the executive sponsor:**
- Work with champion to identify the right executive (CIO, CTO, VP Eng, etc.)
- Present the business case: "Your company has X users across Y teams. Here is what consolidating on an enterprise plan looks like."
- Address enterprise requirements: security, compliance, admin, SLA

**Navigate procurement:**
- Provide all security documentation upfront (do not wait to be asked)
- Have enterprise legal terms ready
- Offer dedicated implementation support
- Provide migration plan from individual/team plans to enterprise

**Negotiation considerations:**
- Enterprise discount should be justified by volume commitment
- Multi-year discounts for commitment (2-3 year agreements)
- Include success milestones and expansion mechanisms
- Define the expansion framework (how pricing scales with growth)

### Success Metrics
- Time from first user to enterprise deal (months)
- Enterprise conversion rate for accounts with X+ users
- Average enterprise deal size
- Enterprise deal cycle time (from first sales touch to close)
- Bottom-up revenue before enterprise deal (validates product value)

## Playbook Summary by Stage

| Stage | Primary Action | Sales Involvement | Key Metric |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|------------|
| Land | Product + onboarding deliver value | None (self-serve) | Activation rate |
| Expand Horizontal | Organic spread, champion support | Light (resources, community) | Departments adopted |
| Expand Vertical | Upgrade triggered by limits/needs | Medium (SDR/AE outreach) | Upgrade conversion rate |
| Enterprise Conversion | Formalize relationship | Heavy (AE + SE + exec engagement) | Enterprise deal size |

---

## Common Expansion Blockers

| Blocker | Symptoms | Resolution |
|---------|----------|------------|
| No champion | Adoption is distributed, no one advocates | Identify and nurture the most active admin/power user |
| Shadow IT | Teams using product without IT approval | Proactively offer security review and IT partnership |
| Budget fragmentation | Each team pays separately, no one sees total spend | Show consolidated spend analysis to finance |
| Competitor incumbent | Company has an existing enterprise contract with competitor | Focus on differentiating value and coexistence, then displacement at renewal |
| Procurement fatigue | Company has complex vendor onboarding | Simplify the procurement experience, provide all docs upfront |
| Champion departure | Key advocate leaves the company | Ensure multiple champions exist; engage at team/org level, not just individual |
