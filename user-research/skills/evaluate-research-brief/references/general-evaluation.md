# General Research Brief Evaluation Reference

Synthesized from The Mom Test (Fitzpatrick), Deploy Empathy (Hansen), and Just Enough Research (Hall).

---

## Evaluation Criteria

### 1. Research Question Sharpness

**PASS if:**
- The question is specific enough to produce a yes/no or a concrete decision
- You can name the business decision this research will change
- The question asks about a specific problem, not "what do users think"
- Uses outcome verbs: "describe," "evaluate," "identify," "analyze" — NOT "understand," "explore," "learn about"
- You can tell when the question has been answered (falsifiable/completable)
- Sub-questions listed if the problem has parts

**FAIL if:**
- The question is exploratory without boundaries (e.g., "understand our users")
- No named business decision will change based on findings
- The question asks customers to predict future behavior ("Would you use this?") or design features ("What do you need?")
- Problem statement contains a solution ("We will understand how users want to use our new dashboard feature" — you've already decided on a dashboard)
- Open-ended verbs ("explore," "understand") signal infinite scope

**Challenge questions:**
- If the answer to this question is X, what will you do differently? If Y? If there's no action difference, the question doesn't matter.
- Are you fishing for validation instead of seeking disconfirming data? (Mom Test)
- Is this what people actually did, or what they said they'd do?

---

### 2. Big 3 Questions Quality (Mom Test)

| Test | Pass | Fail |
|---|---|---|
| Could an unexpected answer reshape or kill the business? | The question targets a load-bearing assumption | The answer would be "interesting" but wouldn't change anything |
| Is at least one question scary to ask? | You feel genuine discomfort imagining asking it | Every question feels safe and comfortable |
| Are questions about their life, not your idea? | "How do you currently solve X?" | "Do you think our idea is good?" |
| Are questions about specifics in the past, not hypotheticals? | "When's the last time this happened?" | "Would you ever use something that...?" |
| Do questions pass The Mom Test? | Even your mom couldn't lie to you in response | The question invites compliments or opinion |

**Red flag:** If none of your 3 questions could destroy the idea, you're fishing for validation, not seeking truth.

---

### 3. Segment & Participant Definition

**PASS if:**
- It's a who-where pair: "Non-native PhD students — find via department advisors"
- Could find 5 of these people this week
- All people in this segment would have the same problem
- Segment sliced by behavior, not just demographics
- Participants defined by specific, filterable criteria (time-based, revenue-based, behavior-based)
- Target count is 5-15 per discrete question
- Diversity explicitly considered (roles, demographics, accessibility needs)
- Behavioral screening used — screener filters on behaviors, not just demographics
- Decoy questions in screener (doesn't telegraph the "right" answers)

**FAIL if:**
- "Our target is busy professionals" — can't have conversations you can't find people for
- Participant definition is just "our users" or "potential customers"
- No stop rule is defined (when to stop interviewing)
- Only one type of customer included (e.g., only churned without happy customers)
- Screening only on demographics — "Women aged 25-34" is a marketing segment, not a design target
- No screener at all

**Challenge questions:**
- Are you only talking to people who will confirm your hypothesis?
- Are you including people with disabilities?
- For cancellation research: Are you balancing with happy customer interviews?

---

### 4. Risk Map Quality

| Test | Pass | Fail |
|---|---|---|
| Are both market risk and product risk identified? | Explicit list of each | Only one type acknowledged |
| Is the elephant in the room named? | Brief includes the question everyone avoids | Conspicuous absence of the hardest question |
| Are product risks acknowledged as not fully conversation-solvable? | Brief notes where building/testing must supplement conversations | Brief assumes conversations will validate everything |
| Has the team answered "If this fails, why?" | Specific failure scenarios listed | Generic "it might not work" |
| Assumptions ranked by risk | Identifies which wrong assumptions carry the highest cost | Everything investigated with equal priority |
| Cost of being wrong articulated | "If wrong about X, we lose Y weeks/dollars/reputation" | Just "we might be wrong" |
| Connected to business goals | Research questions tie directly to business objectives | No connection between questions and decisions |

---

### 5. Beliefs & Hypotheses

| Test | Pass | Fail |
|---|---|---|
| Hypotheses written as beliefs, not facts | "We believe X because Y" | "Our customers need X" (stated as given) |
| Could you be proven wrong? | Each belief has a clear contradiction scenario | Beliefs so vague any data confirms them |
| Desk research done first | Questions Google can't answer | Questions whose answers are on the first page of a search |

**Red flag:** If every possible conversation outcome would be interpreted as "good news," the beliefs aren't falsifiable.

---

### 6. Interview Type & Approach Fit

**PASS if:**
- Interview type matches the research question:
  - Does this problem exist? How painful? → Discovery
  - Why did people become customers? → Switch
  - Why do people stay? What use cases work? → Long-time customer
  - Why are people leaving? → Cancellation
  - Is this usable? Does the value land? → Interactive
  - Which problems are most acute? → Card sorting
- Research type matches the question:
  - Generative for "what's the problem?"
  - Descriptive for "how does this work?"
  - Evaluative for "does our solution work?"
  - Causal for "why is this happening?"
- Method matches resources (time, budget, team)
- If discovery: script probes the problem, not the solution
- If cancellation: explicitly states this is not a "save" call

**FAIL if:**
- Type doesn't match the question
- Evaluative research when the team hasn't done generative first (testing a solution to an undefined problem)
- Method chosen for familiarity ("We always do usability testing")
- Cancellation interviews planned as first-ever interviews (start with happy customers)

---

### 7. Core Questions Coverage (Deploy Empathy)

**PASS if the brief's questions map to all 7 core questions:**

| Core Question | Covered? |
|---|---|
| What are they trying to do overall? (Goal) | |
| What are all the steps in that process? | |
| Where are they now? | |
| Where does the problem fit in their process? | |
| Where do they spend a lot of time or money? | |
| How often do they experience this? | |
| What have they already tried? | |

**FAIL if:**
- Questions ask for predictions ("Would you use...?" / "Would you pay...?")
- Questions ask customers to design solutions ("What features do you need?")
- Questions use loaded language ("What are your pain points?")
- Social and emotional dimensions absent — only functional questions included
- Questions not phrased simply enough for someone outside your industry

---

### 8. Logistics Viability

**PASS if:**
- Format is audio-only (unless interactive interview requires screen share)
- Duration realistic: 30 min default, 20-25 min for cancellation
- Max 2 interviews per day
- Recording permission in plan
- Note capture method defined
- Thank-you note process defined
- Timeline realistic — recruiting time included
- Fallback plans stated
- Data management plan (naming, storage, backup)

**FAIL if:**
- Video planned for non-interactive interviews (reduces openness)
- More than 2 interviews per day
- No recording or note-taking plan
- No contingency for no-shows, multi-attendees, onboarding requests
- No time allocated for analysis

---

### 9. Incentive Appropriateness

| Scenario | Appropriate Incentive |
|---|---|
| Discovery via Reddit/cold outreach | $10-25 gift card |
| Existing happy customers | Swag (stickers, socks, hats) — no gift card |
| Canceled customers | $25 gift card — never swag |
| Screen share / interactive | $10-25 gift card |
| Existing customer with feature request motivation | No incentive needed |

**FAIL if:**
- Cancellation interviews without incentive
- Swag offered to churned customers
- No plan to send incentive promptly

---

### 10. Roles & Accountability

**PASS if:**
- Single point person named
- Interviewer ≠ notetaker (or recording is explicit backup)
- Analyst role has >1 person (solo analysis invites confirmation bias)
- Design team participates in research, not just receives a report
- Stakeholder observation planned

**Red flag:** Researcher will "present findings" to a design team that had no involvement. "Presenting them with the world's most stunning report is far less likely to inspire them to approach their work differently." (Hall)

---

### 11. Bias Controls

**PASS if:**
- Known biases listed with specific countermeasures
- Sponsor identity managed (company name withheld until unavoidable)
- Interview neutrality planned (practice interviews scheduled, leading questions flagged)

**FAIL if:**
- No mention of bias
- Brief says "we will avoid bias" without specifying how
- The person who designed the system is also facilitating the usability test

---

### 12. Recruitment Copy Quality

**PASS if:**
- Copy is short — one sentence per point with line breaks
- Zero promotional or sales language
- Asks for people with recent, concrete experience
- Includes: who you are, time needed, why, when, incentive
- Personalized for the channel

**FAIL if:**
- Copy reads like a sales pitch
- Asks for people interested in a solution rather than people who experience the problem

---

### 13. Analysis Plan

**PASS if:**
- Analysis method defined before interviews begin
- Method matches the research question
- Plan includes waiting for 5 interviews before making decisions
- Plan includes filtering through viable/usable/valuable/feasible (Cagan)

**FAIL if:**
- No analysis plan — just "we'll see what we learn"
- Plan implies acting on a single interview
- No plan to filter findings through business viability
- Analysis conflates customer requests with product decisions

---

### 14. Success Criteria & Commitments

**PASS if:**
- "Done" defined in terms of decisions that can be made, not volume
- Criteria specific: "We can identify the top 3 churn drivers and their acquisition sources"
- Decision rule for inconclusive results (narrow scope, do another round of 5)
- Commitments appropriate to stage: Early = facts; Mid = time/reputation/money commitment; Late = advancement
- Clear next step after the batch

**FAIL if:**
- No success criteria
- Success defined as "completing X interviews"
- No contingency for inconclusive results
- No post-conversation plan

---

### 15. Ethical Soundness

**PASS if:**
- Informed consent process (participants understand goals, recording, data usage)
- No deception beyond necessary (hiding product name is fine; false expectations is not)
- Participant safety considered (no fatigue, no dangerous conditions)
- Vulnerable populations addressed (children require parental consent)
- Overall project ethics questioned ("Is the project itself ethical?")

**FAIL if:**
- No consent process — non-negotiable

## Anti-Pattern Detector

| Anti-pattern | What it looks like | Why it fails |
|---|---|---|
| **Comfort questions** | All 3 questions feel easy and pleasant | You'll collect trivia, not truth |
| **Premature zoom** | Questions drill into feature details before validating the problem | Detailed answers to irrelevant questions |
| **Validation seeking** | Questions structured to confirm, not disconfirm | "Don't you hate when X?" leads witnesses |
| **Unfindable segment** | "Our target is busy professionals" | Can't have conversations you can't find people for |
| **Missing stakeholders** | B2B brief targeting only the user, not budget holder | "Validate" with people who can't buy |
| **All market risk, no product risk** | Brief ignores technical feasibility or growth challenges | False confidence |
| **No scary question** | Every question is about understanding, none about commitment | Friend-zone with zombie leads |

## Scoring Dimensions (General)

| Dimension | Weight | What it measures |
|---|---|---|
| Research Question Sharpness | 12% | Falsifiable, decision-linked, outcome-verbed |
| Big 3 Questions | 10% | Scary, past-focused, Mom-Test-proof |
| Segment & Participants | 12% | Behavior-based, findable, diverse, screened |
| Risk Map | 8% | Both risk types, elephant named, cost articulated |
| Beliefs & Hypotheses | 8% | Explicit, falsifiable, desk-research-first |
| Interview Type & Approach | 8% | Type matches question, method matches resources |
| Core Questions Coverage | 10% | All 7 Deploy Empathy questions mapped |
| Logistics | 6% | Realistic, fallbacks, recording, notes |
| Incentives | 3% | Stage-appropriate, channel-appropriate |
| Roles & Accountability | 5% | Point person, separation of duties, team involvement |
| Bias Controls | 5% | Named, mitigated, sponsor managed |
| Analysis Plan | 5% | Pre-defined, method-matched, business-filtered |
| Success Criteria & Commitments | 5% | Decision-based, stage-appropriate, contingency |
| Ethics | 3% | Consent, safety, project-level ethics |
